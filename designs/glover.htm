  About the Design
Glover's Stegosaurus design is founded on the assumption that a bright color treatment in the tail-spike region might serve as an effective warning to any predator to avoid the powerful defensive tail. A current example of this approach would be the brightly hued, diminutive but very poisonous tree frogs found in today's tropics. Glover chose to utilize his characteristic free-flowing lines applied in a manner that is harmonious with the dinosaur's 3D contours. These lines become apparent to the viewer when seen up close. From a distance, the colors appear softer and more natural but still exciting. Visit John Peter Glover's website.
